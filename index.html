SDG 2 (Sustainable Development Goal 2), also known as "Zero Hunger", aims to end hunger, achieve food security, improve nutrition, and promote sustainable agriculture by 2030.

Hunger is a complex issue with numerous interconnected causes. Some of them are:

1. Poverty and Inequality

  • Poverty prevents people from affording adequate and nutritious food.
  • Inequality in resource distribution, like land, water, and credit, limits access to food and production.

2. Conflict

  • Conflict disrupts food production, displaces populations, and hinders access to markets and aid.
  • Conflict zones often experience higher rates of hunger and malnutrition.

3. Climate Change

  • Extreme weather events like droughts, floods, and heatwaves damage crops, livestock, and infrastructure, leading to food shortages.
  • Climate change impacts livelihoods and forces displacements, increasing vulnerability to hunger.

4. Economic Factors

  • Economic downturns and instability lead to job losses and reduced purchasing power.
  • Food price fluctuations, especially in developing countries, disproportionately affect vulnerable populations.

5. Gender Equality

  • Women and girls often face barriers in accessing food, resources, and education, making them more vulnerable to hunger.
  • They also have unique nutritional needs, especially during pregnancy and breastfeeding, which may not be met.

6. Food Waste

  • Globally, a significant amount of food is wasted, contributing to food insecurity.
  • Inefficient food systems and lack of infrastructure can lead to post-harvest losses.
